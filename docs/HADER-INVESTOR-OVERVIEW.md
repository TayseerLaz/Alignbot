## 1. The one-paragraph summary

**Hader is an AI employee that lives inside a business's WhatsApp.** It answers customer questions instantly, day and night, using only the business's real information — its products, prices, opening hours, services, and policies. It takes orders and bookings, sends marketing campaigns, and hands off to a human the moment something needs a person. The business owner sets it up by filling in simple forms — no technical skill required — and Hader does the rest. For the customer, it feels like chatting with a fast, friendly, always-available member of staff. For the business, it is a 24/7 sales-and-service team that never sleeps, never forgets a price, and costs a fraction of a single employee.

## 2. The problem we solve

Small and medium businesses live and die on customer conversations — and almost all of those conversations now happen on WhatsApp. But the way businesses handle those conversations today is broken:

- **They answer too slowly.** A customer messages at 9pm asking "are you open tomorrow?" or "how much is this?" and gets a reply the next morning — if at all. By then the customer has gone to a competitor.
- **They answer inconsistently.** The owner knows the prices; the part-time staff member does not. Customers get different answers depending on who happens to be holding the phone.
- **They lose sales they never even see.** Most "lost" customers don't complain — they just go quiet. The business has no idea how many sales walked away because nobody replied in time.
- **The owner is the bottleneck.** Replying to the same five questions all day ("Do you deliver?", "What are your hours?", "Do you have this in stock?") eats the owner's time and pulls them away from actually running the business.
- **Existing solutions don't fit.** Big enterprise chatbot tools are expensive, complicated, and built for call-centres. Generic AI tools "make things up" — they'll confidently quote a price that doesn't exist. Neither is safe or simple enough for a café, a clinic, or a corner shop.

**The result:** the average small business is leaving money on the table every single day, simply because it cannot answer fast enough, accurately enough, around the clock.

Messages on WhatsApp are opened at around a **95–98% rate**, versus roughly **20% for email**. Studies repeatedly show that the business that **responds first** wins the majority of the deal — and that response speed expectations are now measured in minutes, not hours. *(Sources: Meta/WhatsApp public figures; messaging open-rate benchmarks; lead-response time studies. Think of Hader as **hiring the perfect front-desk employee** — except this one:

## 4. How it works — the simple version

There are only three things a business does, and only one of them ever again after setup:

**Step 1 — Tell Hader about the business (a one-time setup).**
The owner fills in simple forms — like filling in a profile. They add their products and prices, their services, their opening hours, their locations, their frequently asked questions, and their policies (delivery, refunds, etc.). If they already have this in a spreadsheet, they upload the spreadsheet and Hader imports it in one click. No technical work.

**Step 2 — Connect WhatsApp.**
With a guided, step-by-step setup, the business connects its official WhatsApp number to Hader. From that moment, every message a customer sends can be answered automatically.

**Step 3 — Let it run, and watch.**
Customers message the business as they always have. Hader answers instantly using the information from Step 1. The owner can watch every conversation from a single, clean dashboard, jump in whenever they want, and see exactly how many questions were answered, how many sales were started, and what customers are asking about.

That's it. The "hard" part — the AI, the speed, the reliability, the security — all happens invisibly in the background. **The owner's experience is filling in forms and reading a dashboard.**

## 5. A safety feature that matters to investors: Hader doesn't make things up

The single biggest fear with AI is that it "hallucinates" — that it confidently invents a price, a product, or a promise that isn't real. For a business, one made-up price or false promise can mean a refund, a bad review, or a legal problem.

**Hader is built specifically to prevent this.** Every reply is checked, after it is written, against the business's real catalogue. If Hader mentions a product or a price, our system traces that claim back to the exact entry the business entered — like a footnote citing its source. If anything looks invented or off-script, it is flagged, recorded, and visible to the operator. We keep a full audit trail of every single reply: what the customer asked, what Hader answered, and exactly which piece of the business's own information it used.

This is a genuine technical moat and a major trust advantage. It means Hader can be trusted in settings — clinics, pharmacies, financial services — where a generic chatbot simply cannot be. **"Grounded and accountable" is our promise, and it is built into the product, not bolted on.**

## 6. Everything Hader can do (the full feature list, in business terms)

### Answering customers (the core)
- **24/7 instant replies** to customer questions on WhatsApp, in the customer's language and the business's tone of voice.
- **A shared inbox** where the owner and staff see every conversation in one place, can read what Hader said, and can take over any chat with one tap.
- **Smart hand-off to a human** for anything sensitive, unusual, or that the business has marked as "always send to a person."

### Knowing the business (the knowledge base)
- **Products** — with photos, variations (sizes, colours, options), and prices.
- **Services** — with pricing tiers and the days/times they're available.
- **Business profile** — opening hours, multiple locations, contact details.
- **FAQs and policies** — delivery, refunds, bookings, anything customers ask repeatedly.
- **One-click import** — upload an existing spreadsheet and Hader fills its knowledge automatically.
- **Version history** — every change is saved, and any earlier version can be restored, so nothing is ever lost.

### Selling and booking
- **Taking orders** directly inside the chat (a shopping-cart experience over WhatsApp).
- **Taking bookings and appointments** — ideal for clinics, salons, and services.

### Marketing and growth
- **Broadcasts** — send a promotion or announcement to many customers at once (e.g. "20% off this weekend"), with delivery tracking so the business sees who received and read it.
- **Audiences and segments** — group customers (e.g. "everyone who ordered last month") and message just that group.
- **Automated message sequences** — set up a series of messages that go out over days (e.g. a welcome series, or a "we miss you" nudge), running automatically.
- **Built-in compliance** — automatic handling of opt-outs ("STOP"/"unsubscribe"), respect for customers' time zones and quiet hours, so the business stays on the right side of the rules without thinking about it.

### Insight and control
- **A live dashboard** the owner can customise, showing what's happening at a glance.
- **Analytics** — how many questions answered, conversations started, sales and bookings driven.
- **Full audit trail** of every AI reply and every change, for trust and accountability.
- **Team management** — invite staff, give them the right level of access (owner, editor, view-only).
- **Security and privacy** — each business's data is fully isolated from every other business's, with bank-grade protections and optional two-factor login.

### For the more advanced
- **Connections to other systems** the business already uses, so Hader's knowledge stays up to date automatically.
- **A clean reporting connection** so a business's existing tools can read its catalogue.

> **The headline for an investor:** this is not a single feature. It is a complete operating system for a small business's customer conversations — service, sales, marketing, and insight — all powered by one AI assistant, all set up without any technical skill.

## 7. Real scenarios — how it plays out

### Scenario A — The café at 11pm
A customer messages: *"Are you open tomorrow and do you do oat milk?"*
Hader replies in three seconds with tomorrow's hours and confirms oat milk is available, then asks if they'd like to pre-order for pickup. The owner is asleep. The next morning there's a paid pickup order waiting. **A sale that, last month, would simply have been missed.**

### Scenario B — The dental clinic
A patient asks: *"How much is a cleaning and do you have anything Thursday afternoon?"*
Hader gives the exact price from the clinic's own list, shows Thursday's open slots, and books the appointment — adding the patient's name and number. For anything clinical ("I have a sharp pain, is this serious?"), Hader does **not** guess; it immediately hands off to the clinic's staff. **Front-desk work handled; medical judgement left to humans.**

### Scenario C — The retail store doing a weekend sale
The owner selects "everyone who bought in the last 90 days" and sends a broadcast: *"This weekend only — 20% off, reply ORDER to shop."* Two thousand messages go out, the owner watches the read-and-reply counts climb live, and Hader handles the flood of "is this in my size?" questions individually and instantly — something no human team could do at once. **A campaign and a sales team, from one screen.**

### Scenario D — The salon that's always on the phone
Instead of interrupting a haircut to answer the phone, the stylist lets Hader handle every "how much / when can I come in / do you do balayage" message. Bookings fill in automatically. The owner checks the dashboard at the end of the day and sees twelve appointments booked while they were working. **Staff stay with the customer in front of them.**

### Scenario E — The real-estate or services firm
A lead messages at midnight about a listing. Hader answers the details, qualifies the lead with a couple of questions, and books a viewing — then alerts the agent first thing in the morning with the full conversation already captured. **No more first-thing scramble; every lead is answered while it's hot.**

## 12. Why this is hard to copy

It looks simple on the surface — which is the point. Underneath, Hader has built several things that are genuinely difficult and that took real time to get right:

- **The accuracy-and-grounding system** that stops the AI making things up and proves where every answer came from. This is our biggest trust differentiator and the hardest part to replicate safely.
- **The complete, one-product-many-industries platform** — service, sales, marketing, and insight all working together, not five separate tools stitched together.
- **The "fill-in-a-form, not write-code" simplicity** that lets a non-technical owner go live in days, not months.
- **Rock-solid separation between businesses' data and reliability under load**, so it can be trusted with real customers' conversations at scale.

A competitor can build a chatbot. Building a *trustworthy, accountable, end-to-end, genuinely-simple* one — and earning the reputation that comes with it — is a different and much longer road.
